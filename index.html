All Roads Lead to Leadership: A story that resonsates

The moment I realized I wanted to travel along the path that is management was a moment of unfiltered humbling. Let me back up and tell you about the time my professor had to put me in a place where I would move on to so clearly see my career purpose.

Scene: Junior year, Principles of Marketing course, University of North Carolina at Greensboro. We were assigned a group project where the premise was to create a product and develop a very detailed marketing plan for said product. This marketing plan included a budget, mission statement, and product break down you know the usual.

Background: Well as most college projects go, our group was selected for us by personal selection of the professor. And again as most college projects go you inevitably have the person who does nothing, the person who over-achieves, and the people who are there to agree with any task assigned by the overachiever.

Unfortunately at this stage in my educational journey I was the person in the group who seeked to overachieve and by that effect I usually took lead and dispersed task as they came. What was different about this group was the fact that there were two overachievers instead of one which lead to a collapse in the normal college project heirarchy.

In an effort to minimize a tale that holds a range of minute details I will get to the main points. As we battled from beginning to end on the product we would create, our mission statement, and the overall presentation it became very clear at one point that this group-ship would ultimately lead to a giant discourse most likely resulting in a failing grade.

We finally arrived at the most effective route and that was to involve our professor who could act as the guiding light. It was in this meeting that I not only learned proper conflict management but I also learned that my Type A personality can be off-putting. The reason this advise worked was because we both needed to hear it. We both had never been in a situation where we could not take entire control of a project and the moment had finally come.

Conclusion: This experience does have a productive and life-changing ending in that not only were two Type A's able to reconcile we ended the project with an A and a second place prize. This summary of one of the most influential moments in my college career has successfully molded me into the leader I am today.
